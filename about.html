About the Mapper

PRW Career Mapper is a web based data visualization tool created for researching the career of architect, Paul R. Williams, FAIA. PRW Career Mapper compiles the only three known published lists of Williams' building projects1 and maps them based on date, location, use, and client type. The goal is to allow researchers a means of exploring what patterns, if any, existed in his career.

It is purported that Williams undertook well over a thousand projects. Thus, the database displayed by the site is by no means complete. PRW Career Mapper is intended to serve as a living document under continuous revision. Suggestions, corrections, and additions are welcome.

Notes:
1. The following sources were used to create the project list. Best efforts were taken to reconcile discrepacies between project names and dates though there may still be errors. For the sake of consistency, we have chosen to date projects according to completion of construction.

Henderson, Wesley. "Paul Revere Williams (1894-1980)." In African American Architects: A Biographical Dictionary, edited by Dreck Spurlock Wilson.1865-1945, 447-452. 1st ed. New York: Routledge, 2004.

Hudson, Karen E., and Paul R. Williams. Paul R. Williams, Architect: A Legacy of Style. New York: Rizzoli, 1993.

"Paul Revere Williams Project." Paul Revere Williams Project. Accessed August 20, 2015. http://www.paulrwilliamsproject.org.
